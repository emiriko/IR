A plane lights on monochromatically and immediately after lights off, while
an observer starts moving out from the plane, perpendicularly to it, with
constant proper acceleration. Special relativity predicts that the observer
will see a light circle always in the direction opposite to the plane, without
Doppler effect, and that the circle will seem to progressively shrink to a
point.
