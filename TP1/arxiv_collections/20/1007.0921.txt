Let $H$ be a real separable Hilbert space and $(a_k)_{k\in\mathbb{Z}}$ a
sequence of bounded linear operators from $H$ to $H$. We consider the linear
process $X$ defined for any $k$ in $\mathbb{Z}$ by
$X_k=\sum_{j\in\mathbb{Z}}a_j(\varepsilon_{k-j})$ where
$(\varepsilon_k)_{k\in\mathbb{Z}}$ is a sequence of i.i.d. centered $H$-valued
random variables. We investigate the rate of convergence in the CLT for $X$ and
in particular we obtain the usual Berry-Esseen's bound provided that
$\sum_{j\in\mathbb{Z}}\vert j\vert\|a_j\|_{\mathcal{L}(H)}<+\infty$ and
$\varepsilon_0$ belongs to $L_H^{\infty}$.
