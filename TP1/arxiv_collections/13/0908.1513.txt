This paper is essentially a translation from French of my article \cite{M1}
published in 2003. Let $u\in C([0,T^{\ast}[;L^{3}(\mathbb{R}% ^{3})) $ be a
maximal solution of the Navier-Stokes equations. We prove that $u$ is
$C^{\infty}$ on $]0,T^{\ast}[\times \mathbb{R}^{3}$ and there exists a constant
$\varepsilon_{\ast}>0$ independent of $u$ such that if $T^{\ast}$ is finite
then, for all $\omega \in \overline{S(\mathbb{R%}^{3})}^{B_{\infty
}^{-1,\infty}},$ we have $\overline{\lim_{t\to T^{\ast}}}\Vert u(t)-\omega
\Vert_{\mathbf{B}_{\infty}^{-1,\infty}}\geq \varepsilon_{\ast}. $
